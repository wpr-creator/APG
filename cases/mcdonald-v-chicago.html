McDonald v. City of Chicago
561 U.S. 742 · June 28, 2010 · Justice Samuel Alito
Unit 3 2nd Amendment 14th Amendment ⭐ Most Recent Incorporation
📋
BACKGROUND

Otis McDonald was a 76-year-old retired maintenance worker living in a high-crime neighborhood on Chicago's South Side. He wanted to keep a handgun at home for self-defense but couldn't — Chicago had a near-total ban on handgun possession enacted in 1982. McDonald challenged the ban following the Supreme Court's 2008 ruling in District of Columbia v. Heller, which had held the 2nd Amendment protects an individual right to keep and bear arms. But Heller only applied to federal enclaves like Washington D.C. McDonald asked: does the 2nd Amendment apply to state and local governments too?

The answer required the Court to decide whether to incorporate the 2nd Amendment through the 14th — the same selective incorporation process used for the 1st, 4th, 5th, and 6th Amendments over the previous century.

❓
CONSTITUTIONAL QUESTION
Does the 2nd Amendment right to keep and bear arms, recognized in Heller as an individual right, apply to state and local governments through the 14th Amendment's Due Process Clause?
⚖️
THE RULING
✓ 2nd Amendment applies to states — 5 to 4
Majority: Alito · Roberts · Scalia · Kennedy · Thomas  |  Dissent: Stevens · Ginsburg · Breyer · Sotomayor
"It is clear that the Framers and ratifiers of the Fourteenth Amendment counted the right to keep and bear arms among those fundamental rights necessary to our system of ordered liberty."
The Court held the 2nd Amendment is incorporated against the states and localities through the 14th Amendment's Due Process Clause. This means state and local governments — not just the federal government — are prohibited from infringing the individual right to keep and bear arms for self-defense in the home recognized in Heller. Chicago's handgun ban was struck down. McDonald is the most recent major selective incorporation case and demonstrates that the process is still ongoing — rights can still be incorporated in the 21st century.
📐
PRINCIPLE ESTABLISHED
2nd Amendment Applies to All Levels of Government — Selective Incorporation Complete
The individual right to keep and bear arms for self-defense (established in Heller 2008) applies to state and local governments through the 14th Amendment's Due Process Clause. State and local governments may regulate firearms but may not ban them entirely for law-abiding citizens in their homes. McDonald is significant beyond its 2nd Amendment holding — it demonstrates that selective incorporation is a continuing process. Rights that seem well-established as federal-only protections can still be incorporated against states decades later.
🔗
LEGACY & CONNECTIONS
  • Heller (2008)
    Precursor    McDonald could not have been decided without Heller. Heller established there IS an individual 2nd Amendment right; McDonald established it applies to states. Know both cases and how they work together.
  • Selective Incorporation
    Process    McDonald sits alongside Gideon (6th Amendment), Mapp v. Ohio (4th Amendment), and Engel (1st Amendment) as examples of the selective incorporation process. The 14th Amendment's Due Process Clause has been the vehicle for applying nearly all of the Bill of Rights to state governments.
  • Bruen (2022)
    Aftermath    New York State Rifle & Pistol Association v. Bruen (2022) strengthened McDonald further — striking down New York's concealed carry licensing law and establishing a new "historical tradition" test for evaluating gun regulations. McDonald opened the door; Bruen widened it considerably.
  • Gun Regulation
    After McDonald    McDonald doesn't prevent all gun regulation — it prevents banning guns for law-abiding citizens in their homes. States and localities may regulate storage, carrying, sales, and specific weapon types. The exact contours of what regulations are permissible are still being litigated in lower courts.
⭐ AP EXAM TIP
McDonald is tested as the most recent selective incorporation case AND as the case that applied the 2nd Amendment to states. Two things to always say about McDonald: (1) it incorporated the 2nd Amendment through the 14th Amendment's Due Process Clause, and (2) it built directly on Heller (2008). FRQ scenarios about state gun laws → McDonald. Questions about selective incorporation → McDonald is the modern example.

Common Mistakes
✗Confusing McDonald with Heller — Heller applied the 2nd Amendment to federal law; McDonald applied it to states.
✗Thinking McDonald makes all gun regulations unconstitutional — it only prohibits complete bans for law-abiding citizens.
✗Forgetting this is a selective incorporation case — always mention the 14th Amendment Due Process Clause.
