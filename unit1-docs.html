Foundational Documents
8 required documents · Select one to read with full annotations
🦅
Declaration of Independence
Jefferson · 1776
Natural rights, social contract, right of revolution. The philosophical foundation of the American republic — and a legal case for independence built on 27 specific grievances.
Read with Annotations →
📜
Articles of Confederation
Continental Congress · 1781
America's first constitution — and a cautionary tale. No power to tax, no executive, no courts. Know what it could NOT do as much as what it could.
Read with Annotations →
⚖️
U.S. Constitution
Constitutional Convention · 1787
Required portions only: Preamble, Articles I–III, Supremacy Clause, N&P Clause, key amendments. Every clause that generated a landmark case.
Read with Annotations →
✍️
Federalist No. 10
Madison · 1787 · ⭐ Most Tested
Factions are inevitable in a free society — but a large republic with many competing interests prevents any one faction from dominating. The origin of American pluralism.
Read with Annotations →
✍️
Federalist No. 51
Madison · 1788 · ⭐ Most Tested
"Ambition must counteract ambition." "If men were angels, no government would be necessary." The definitive defense of separation of powers and checks and balances.
Read with Annotations →
⚡
Brutus No. 1
Yates (attr.) · 1787 · Anti-Federalist
The N&P and Supremacy Clauses will give the federal government unlimited power to destroy the states. The best counterargument to Madison — and he was right about a lot of it.
Read with Annotations →
✍️
Federalist No. 39
Madison · 1788
Is the new government national or federal? Madison's answer: both — a compound republic, partly national and partly federal in its foundation, powers, and operation.
Read with Annotations →
💰
The Wealth of Nations
Adam Smith · 1776
The "invisible hand," free markets, and self-interest as the engine of prosperity. The economic philosophy underpinning the Framers' vision of limited government.
