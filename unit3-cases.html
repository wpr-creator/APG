Unit 3 · Civil Liberties & Civil Rights
Required SCOTUS Cases
8 required cases · Select one for full case breakdown
1919
1st Amend.
Schenck v. United States
Can government restrict speech creating a "clear and present danger"?
Yes — Holmes established the clear and present danger test. Anti-draft leaflets not protected.
1962
Est. Clause
Engel v. Vitale
Does state-sponsored prayer in public schools violate the Establishment Clause?
Yes — even a voluntary, non-denominational state-written prayer crosses the line.
1969
1st Amend.
Tinker v. Des Moines
Do students shed their constitutional rights at the schoolhouse gate?
No — schools can only restrict speech causing "substantial disruption." Silent armbands don't qualify.
1971
1st Amend.
New York Times v. United States
Can government stop a newspaper from publishing the Pentagon Papers?
No — prior restraint is the most serious First Amendment violation. Government couldn't meet the burden.
1972
Free Exercise
Wisconsin v. Yoder
Can the state compel Amish families to keep children in school past 8th grade?
No — compulsory education law violated the Free Exercise Clause. State interest didn't outweigh religious practice.
1963
6th Amend.
Gideon v. Wainwright
Must states provide a lawyer to felony defendants who can't afford one?
Yes — Gideon's handwritten appeal won unanimously. Right to counsel is fundamental to a fair trial.
1954
Eq. Protection
Brown v. Board of Education
Does racial segregation in public schools violate the Equal Protection Clause?
Yes — "Separate but equal is inherently unequal." Unanimous. Overturned Plessy (1896).
2010
2nd & 14th
McDonald v. City of Chicago
Does the 2nd Amendment apply to state and local governments?
Yes — most recent major selective incorporation case. 2nd Amendment applies to all levels of government.
