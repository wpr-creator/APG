Foundational Documents
3 required documents · Select one to read with full annotations
📜
Emancipation Proclamation
Lincoln · January 1, 1863
Issued under commander-in-chief authority — freed enslaved people in Confederate states only. Know what it did AND what it didn't do. Set the stage for the 13th Amendment.
Read with Annotations →
✉️
Letter from Birmingham Jail
Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. · April 16, 1963
Written from jail in response to white clergy calling protests "unwise and untimely." The most precisely argued defense of civil disobedience ever written. Read it as argument.
Read with Annotations →
🎖️
The Gettysburg Address
Lincoln · November 19, 1863
Two minutes, 272 words. Reframes the Civil War as a test of whether a nation "conceived in liberty" and dedicated to equality can endure — and redefines the Declaration as the nation's founding promise.
